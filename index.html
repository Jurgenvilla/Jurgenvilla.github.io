I was born on November 28th 1991 in Medellín, Colombia. I obtained a bachelor of science in Astronomy at the University of Antioquia in Medellín. Currently I am a Ph.D. candidate at LAM (Laboratoire d'Astrophysique de Marseille). During my bachelor I focused my research on investigating dark matter halo properties and its connections to observational parameters derived from Sérsic's profile for disk galaxies using observations from the SDSS. In Leiden, as part of my first year master, I studied emission lines as part of a blind detection survey in submillimeter galaxies in The Chandra Deep Field South (CDF-S) using ALMA data. During my master major project (thesis) I worked on ringed-exoplanet simulations to constrain possible observable transits using Super-WASP and Gaia DR2 data. At the moment I am a first-year Ph.D. candidate at LAM under the supervision of Dr. Véronique Buat. I study dust sttenuation in galaxies through Spectral energy Distribution (SED) fitting using CIGALE (Code Investigating GALaxy Emission). We work mainly on possible modifications of the existent attenuation laws for galaxies using data of the COSMOS and GOODS-S fields in order to enrich our knowledge on how dust affects our physical parametrization of galaxies. In addtion, we fit SEDs not only using photometric data as the traditional methods but including emission line fluxes. As a second project, I provide mock sample spectra produced using CIGALE to the MOONS extragalactic working group. MOONS will be a multiobject spectrograph working at one of the VLT telescopes giving its first light around 2021. One of my hobbies is to walk around and take pictures of nature, building and landscape. I really enjoy performing night telescope observation and showing around the night sky to new people.

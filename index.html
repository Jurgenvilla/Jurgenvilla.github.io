I am a Colombian astronomer from Medellín, Colombia currently wrapping up my Ph.D. at Laboratoire d'Astrophysique de Marseille in France. I work on galaxies, studying their evolution and formation and in particular dust attenuation and star-formation.

My work consists mainly on analyzing spectro-photometric samples of galaxies at cosmic noon. For this I use CIGALE to perform SED fitting with an updated version of the nebular module. I am mostly interested in using emission lines like Ha, Hb, [NII], [SII] and [OIII] along with samples covering ultra-violet to infrared photometry. My studies focous on deriving accurate estimates of stellar mass, star formation rate, and dust attenuation for the emission lines.

I work for the MOONS scientific team modeling mock spectra samples with CIGALE to test the simulation software and check the physical parameter estimation.

Besides my work in Astronomy, I like walking around and taking pictures of nature, building and landscape. Biking and hiking are my favorite sports. I really enjoy watching the nightsky with my telescope and explaining to people about the constellations.

List of Projects

Mock spectra catalog simulations
As part of the MOONS collaboration I am in charge of creating mock spectra samples using CIGALE. Currently, I am SED fitting galaxies in the COSMOS field using the COSMOS2015 catalog. The fit only includes photometry but I use CIGALE to model continuum, and continuum+lines. I have been testing this on a small sample of galaxies with FMOS observations to check the accuracy of the emission line modeling. Extending the results to the whole sample of galaxies in the COSMOS field and other fields is an ongoing process.

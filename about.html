Jorge Andrés Villa Vélez

I was born on November 28th 1991 in Medellín, Colombia.

I obtained a bachelor of science in Astronomy degree at the University of Antioquia. Currently I am an Astronomy master student in the Leiden University. During my bachelor I did my research focused on dark matter halo properties and its connection to observational parameters of Sérsic's profile for disk galaxies.

In my first year master, I studied the emission lines for submillimeter galaxies in The Chandra Deep Field South (CDF-S) using ALMA data. During my master major project (thesis) I worked on ringed-exoplanet simulations to constrain possible transits observed Gaia data.

At the moment I am a first-year Ph.D. candidate at LAM in Marseille, France. I study dust sttenuation in galaxies through SED fitting using CIGALE. We work on possible modifications of the attenuation laws using data of the COSMOS and GOODS-S fields. In addtion, we fit SEDs not only using photometric data but inclusing emission lines. As a second project, I provide mock sample spectra produced using CIGALE to the MOONS extragalactic working group. MOONS will be a multiobject spectrograph working at one of the VLT telescopes giving its first light around 2021.

One of my hobbies is to walk around and take pictures of nature, building and landscape. I really enjoy performing night telescope observation and showing around the night sky to new people.
